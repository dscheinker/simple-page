Critical Drug Interactions in Pediatric Care

Azole Antifungals and Immunosuppressants

Critical Interaction: Significant increases in immunosuppressant blood levels when combined with azole antifungals (especially voriconazole and posaconazole)
Management:
  • Reduce immunosuppressant dose by 30-50% when initiating azole therapy
  • Monitor immunosuppressant levels every 48-72 hours until stable
  • Consider alternative antifungal classes in high-risk patients
Source: Triazole antifungal drug interactions-practical considerations for excellent prescribing
Veringa A, et al. Br J Clin Pharmacol. 2024 Jan 31. PMID: 38629250

Oncology Drug Interactions

Critical Interaction: Multiple drug interactions in onco-hematological pediatric patients, particularly with supportive care medications
Management:
  • Regular monitoring of drug levels for medications with narrow therapeutic windows
  • Consider drug interaction databases for comprehensive screening
  • Implement standardized monitoring protocols
Source: Pharmacoepidemiological study of drug-drug interactions in onco-hematological pediatric patients
Fernández de Palencia Espinosa MA, et al. Med Clin (Barc). 2014 Dec 9;143(11):478-83. PMID: 25204258

Transplant Medication Interactions

Critical Interaction: Complex interactions between immunosuppressants and antifungal prophylaxis in pediatric transplant recipients
Management:
  • Carefully evaluate transition between different azole antifungals
  • Adjust immunosuppressant dosing proactively
  • Regular therapeutic drug monitoring
Source: Pipeline of Novel Antifungals for Invasive Fungal Disease in Transplant Recipients: A Pediatric Perspective
Fisher BT, et al. Journal of the Pediatric Infectious Diseases Society, Volume 13, Issue Supplement_1, March 2024, Pages S68–S77
